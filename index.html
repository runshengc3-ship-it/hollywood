Curatorial Manifesto | Thematic Statement

An Entirely Confident Culture Never Speaks in Only One Voice

When Chinese cinema goes out into the world, what it carries is not a single face, but cinematic narratives of a thousand forms and appearances. With the theme “Telling Chinese Stories to the World: Cultural Soft Power and Confident Echoes on Screen,” this film festival carefully selects five outstanding works spanning kung fu action, science-fiction disaster, modern warfare, historical drama, and fantasy romance. Like five beams of light in different colors, they jointly tell the world of a China that is diverse, three-dimensional, and confident.

This cultural confidence has completely different faces.

It is the Wing Chun stance of a grandmaster, using the Eastern wisdom of “stopping war through martial virtue” to speak to the world and demonstrate the soft power of traditional culture. It is the ten thousand engines pushing the Earth across the frozen plain, responding to the shared destiny of humankind with the deep feeling of “taking our home with us,” and showing the universal force of Eastern values. It is the figure of a veteran soldier standing guard at the national border, interpreting the responsibility of a major country through the protection of compatriots and revealing the persuasive power of the national spirit. It is the smiles and tears of seven ordinary people, redefining grand narrative through individual warmth and telling a Chinese story in a way that is gentle yet resilient. It is an Eastern love that crosses the boundary between human and spirit, questioning universal emotion through reflections on appearance and sincerity, and completing a cross-national dialogue of culture in the most poetic way.

Five genres, five voices, and five on-screen echoes of cultural soft power. These films no longer wait passively to be gazed at. Instead, they actively enter the mainstream track of global cinema: using kung fu to converse with tradition, science fiction to embrace the future, war to write responsibility, history to warm the human heart, and love to cross boundaries. This is precisely the core of soft power as described by Joseph Nye: not imposing influence through coercion, but winning sincere resonance through attraction.

To tell Chinese stories to the world is to show the world cultural confidence. This year’s film festival invites you to walk into the cinema, listen to these five stories, and witness a China that is speaking with ease and echoing with confidence.

Five Films and Reasons for Selection

The Official Selection

Kung Fu Action 2008

Ip Man

Director: Wilson Yip | Genre: Kung Fu / Biographical

Soft Power of Martial Spirit and Cultural Dignity

In Foshan in the 1930s, the Japanese army invades and the homeland falls. Ip Man, the grandmaster of Wing Chun, descends from a wealthy gentleman into extreme poverty, yet at a moment of national peril he steps forward and uses his two fists to defend the dignity of his people. From the quiet practice before the wooden dummy to the resolute battle in the arena against a Japanese officer, Ip Man tells the world through Wing Chun that true strength lies not in knocking down an opponent, but in stopping violence through martial virtue and winning respect through moral character.

Within the framework of a commercial kung fu film, this work injects a distinctly Eastern heroism: restraint, benevolence, righteousness, and composure. It allows kung fu to become not an exotic Eastern spectacle, but a narrative subject carrying cultural soft power. When Ip Man rolls up his sleeves and calmly faces the challenge in the arena, what he shows the world is the martial philosophy of “stopping war through martial virtue” in traditional Chinese culture. This spiritual force of winning others through virtue endures far longer and inspires far more deeply than the destructive force of fists and kicks.

International Presentation

The Ip Man series has been released in more than eighty countries and regions around the world, becoming the most internationally recognizable Chinese kung fu intellectual property after Bruce Lee and Jackie Chan. Through the philosophy of Eastern martial arts, it completes a cultural dialogue with global action-film audiences and stands as an exemplary work in which the soft power of traditional culture tells Chinese stories to the world.

Science-Fiction Disaster 2019

The Wandering Earth

Director: Frant Gwo | Genre: Science Fiction / Disaster

Soft Power of Homeland Feeling and Humanity’s Shared Destiny

The sun is about to expand and swallow the Earth. Humanity decides to launch the Wandering Earth Project, building ten thousand engines on the surface of the planet to push the entire Earth out of the solar system. When Western science fiction is accustomed to depicting elites boarding spaceships and seeking a new home, this milestone of Chinese science fiction offers an entirely different plan: taking the Earth with us.

This setting is rooted in the deep attachment of Chinese civilization to the land, yet through the globally legible language of hard science fiction it touches a shared emotional question for all humankind: what is home, and what makes a home? The film shows the world a uniquely Chinese answer. In the face of crisis, it does not abandon and does not give up; it lives and dies together with home. This is the deeper core of Chinese cultural soft power: not conquering through economic or military strength, but inspiring and resonating through a value system built around a community with a shared future.

International Presentation

When The Wandering Earth was released in North America, Australia, and New Zealand, it sparked widespread discussion. Netflix acquired its global streaming rights, and international media praised it as the beginning of “the first year of Chinese science-fiction cinema.” It proves that Chinese stories can use the most international language of genre film to convey the most distinctive cultural values, making it a successful practice in telling Chinese stories to the world.

Modern Warfare 2015

Wolf Warrior

Director: Wu Jing | Genre: Action / War

Soft Power of Major-Country Responsibility and the Spirit of Protection

Leng Feng, a retired special forces soldier, encounters the incursion of foreign mercenaries during a border exercise. As his comrades and the homeland come under threat, he does not choose to retreat. Instead, with an iron will as his blade, he blocks the enemy at the national border. “Whoever offends China will be punished no matter how far away” runs through the film as a line that declares, within the framework of a standard action blockbuster, a narrative posture that no longer avoids the confidence of a major country.

The Chinese story this film tells the world is not a narrative of conquest through swaggering force, but a value narrative of protection and responsibility. Leng Feng fights not in order to invade, but in order to protect; not to expand, but to defend. This cultural soft power, centered on protection, shapes the image of a responsible and trustworthy major country. China’s strength is presented as the source of every ordinary citizen’s sense of security, not as a threat to others.

International Presentation

Wolf Warrior became the first Chinese-language film to enter the global top one hundred box-office chart, while its sequel Wolf Warrior 2 set an Asian box-office record with 870 million US dollars. Using the international grammar of action cinema, it achieved the global circulation of a Chinese-style heroic narrative and showed the world both the cultural confidence and the market appeal of Chinese film.

Historical Drama 2019

My People, My Country

Directors: Chen Kaige, Zhang Yibai, Guan Hu, Xue Xiaolu, Xu Zheng, Ning Hao, Wen Muye | Genre: Drama / Anthology

Soft Power of Individual Warmth Within Collective Memory

Seven historical moments, seven groups of ordinary people. On the eve of the founding ceremony of the People’s Republic, an engineer stays awake all night for the electric flag-raising device. When the women’s volleyball team wins the championship, a Shanghai alley erupts over a single cheer. During the opening ceremony of the Beijing Olympics, one ticket links together the joys and sorrows of ordinary people. Created jointly by seven directors, this anthology film turns the grand narrative of seventy years of New China into the smiles and tears found in streets, alleys, and everyday life, using a prose-poem-like touch.

The cultural confidence the film shows to the world is a power that is gentle yet profound. It no longer tries to defend itself through grand narrative by arguing “how good we are,” but calmly presents “who we are” instead: ordinary people who live earnestly and warm one another within the current of history. This narrative posture, “witnessing the extraordinary through the ordinary,” is precisely the most moving form of cultural soft power. It is not top-down preaching, but a sharing with the world at eye level.

International Presentation

When the film was released in North America, Europe, and Australia, its unique narrative structure and universally legible emotional expression sparked curiosity and understanding among overseas Chinese audiences as well as foreign viewers regarding contemporary Chinese history. It tells the world a Chinese story full of warmth: the greatness of the nation can be seen in the smiles and tears of ordinary people.

Fantasy Romance 2008

Painted Skin

Director: Gordon Chan | Genre: Fantasy / Romance

Soft Power of Eastern Emotional Philosophy and Human Dilemma

In the desert beneath a lone plume of smoke, the fox spirit Xiaowei falls in love with General Wang Sheng. Yet Wang Sheng already has a wife, Peirong. In order to obtain this love, Xiaowei does not hesitate to use demonic arts to bewitch human hearts, while Peirong is willing to transform herself into a white-haired demon in order to prove her innocence. When outward appearance and true feeling no longer remain united, and when love and possession become hard to distinguish, an Eastern inquiry into the meaning of love slowly unfolds within a magical visual spectacle.

Adapted from Strange Tales from a Chinese Studio, the film wraps profound reflections from traditional Chinese culture on emotion and righteousness, desire and virtue, inside the shell of a strange and supernatural romance. In the end, Xiaowei withdraws her outstretched hand and dissolves into mist and glow. This Eastern view of love, in which love means fulfillment rather than possession, shows the world a unique Chinese philosophy of feeling. The film proves that Chinese cultural soft power does not always need to appeal to grand historical narrative. A singular romance crossing the boundary between human and spirit can likewise complete a cross-cultural emotional dialogue in the most poetic way.

International Presentation

Painted Skin triggered phenomenal enthusiasm in East Asian and Southeast Asian markets and successfully entered mainstream markets in Europe and North America. Through the perfect combination of Eastern aesthetics and universal emotional dilemmas, it shows the world a Chinese story that can remain rooted in tradition while still touching feelings shared by all humanity.

Narrative Arc of the Five Films

A Complete Spectrum Across Screen Space

From the martial arts hall in Foshan to an ice-bound Earth, from China’s border to Beijing’s alleyways, and then to a strange romance crossing the boundary between human and spirit in the depths of the desert, these five films of strikingly different genres together form a complete spectrum: grounded in traditional martial virtue, illuminated by future imagination, written through major-country responsibility, carried by individual warmth, and suffused with Eastern feeling.

Together, they answer one question of the age: what is cultural soft power? It is using the wisdom of martial arts in kung fu, the deep feeling for home in science fiction, the spirit of protection and responsibility in war, the individual warmth in history, and the philosophy of fulfillment in love to attract, inspire, and resonate.

This is the confident echo on screen of telling Chinese stories to the world.

Kung Fu Martial wisdom and dignity become a language of dialogue.
Science Fiction Home and shared destiny become universal emotional force.
War Protection and responsibility define the image of strength.
History Ordinary lives carry the warmth of collective memory.
Love Fulfillment rather than possession opens poetic cross-cultural feeling.
